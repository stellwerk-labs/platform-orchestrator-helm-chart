1. Look at the content of the seaweeddfs filer UI:

  Porforward the SeaweedFS filer service to your local machine:
  kubectl port-forward -n platform-orchestrator svc/platform-orchestrator-seaweed-filer 8888:8888

  Then open your browser and navigate to:
  http://localhost:8888/

2. Access the bucket via AWS CLI:

   Porforward the SeaweedFS filer service to your local machine:
   kubectl port-forward -n platform-orchestrator svc/platform-orchestrator-seaweed-filer 8333:8333

   Then run the following command (replace YOUR_ADMIN_KEY and YOUR_ADMIN_SECRET with your actual credentials, they are stored in the Kubernetes secret `platform-orchestrator/platform-orchestrator-s3-admin-credentials`):
   AWS_ACCESS_KEY_ID=YOUR_ADMIN_KEY AWS_SECRET_ACCESS_KEY=YOUR_ADMIN_SECRET aws --endpoint-url=http://localhost:8333 s3 ls s3://deployment-runner-logs --no-verify-ssl

3. Portforward the Master Service to perform operations:
    kubectl port-forward -n platform-orchestrator svc/platform-orchestrator-seaweed-master 9333:9333

    You can now access the SeaweedFS Master UI at:
    http://localhost:9333/ui/index.html

    Delete the created bucket by running:
    curl "http://localhost:9333/col/delete?collection=deployment-runner-logs"
